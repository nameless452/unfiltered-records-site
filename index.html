03 · Spacing & Scale

Give the mark room.

Clear space protects recognition. Keep surrounding text, images and edges outside the exclusion zone. Never let the wordmark become too small to read.

XX

Minimum clear space = the diameter of the centre record label, shown as X, on every side of the complete lockup.

Icon minimum

Digital: 32 px wide · Print: 10 mm wide. Below this, use no extra detail or effects.

Lockup minimum

Digital: 180 px wide · Print: 40 mm wide. Ensure “Records” remains fully legible.

Badge minimum

Digital: 96 px wide · Print: 24 mm wide. Best for vertical and square formats.

06 · Visual Language

Movement with discipline.

The system should feel musical without becoming literal. Build with rotation, sharp cuts, circular rhythm, bold framing and strong negative space.

Rotational pattern

Use cropped circular forms to suggest groove, momentum and a record in motion.

Sharp cuts

Borrow the angular edge language from the icon. Use sparingly as framing, not decoration.

Concentric rhythm

Repeating circles can guide focus toward an artist, track title or release date.

Structured layout

Use a strong grid, compact metadata and generous breathing room around big statements.

07 · Tone of Voice

Say it like the music sounds.

Copy should be immediate, confident and human. The label speaks with conviction—not hype, filler or industry clichés.

Voice principles

DirectLead with the point. Short sentences. Active language.
Artist-firstMake the artist and the work the centre of every message.
SpecificUse real details, not vague superlatives.
UnforcedNever sound like a corporate campaign trying to be underground.

09 · Logo Use

Keep it intact.

Consistency creates recognition. Always use approved artwork and colour combinations. Never redraw, distort or decorate the logo.

Correct use
Use approved colours, preserve proportions, maintain clear space and choose a background with strong contrast.
Incorrect use
Do not recolour, skew, rotate, stretch, add shadows, place on noisy imagery or separate elements of the lockup.
